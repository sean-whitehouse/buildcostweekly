Bid Check

A second set of eyes on your bid — before the customer sees it.

You've done the takeoff, priced the job, and there's a nagging feeling you forgot something. Usually the feeling is right: most small-job bids don't fail on bad math — they fail on missing line items. Bid Check is a working construction estimator (with serious AI tooling behind him) going through your bid the way a hostile competitor would, and handing you the findings before they cost you money.

What you get

  • Missing-item sweep — the systematic checklist pass: delivery, consumables, tax, permits, disposal, equipment, mobilization, the exclusions you forgot to write down.
  • Structure check — quantities → waste factors → loaded labor → overhead → margin. Each layer flagged as sound, thin, or missing.
  • Current-market price flags — line items that look stale against this week's material market (the same data behind our weekly snapshot), and whether your bid's validity window matches the market's mood.
  • A written report — findings ranked by dollar impact, in plain English, delivered within 3 business days.

Price and capacity

$149 flat per bid (residential/light-commercial bids up to ~$250K contract value — bigger than that, ask first). We take 2 bids per week, period — this service is done carefully by one person, not farmed out. If the week is full you'll be offered the next open slot.

How to submit

Email your bid (PDF or spreadsheet), the plans or scope description you bid from, and your rough labor rate to [SERVICE EMAIL — pending setup] with subject "Bid Check". You'll get back a confirmation, the payment link, and your delivery date. Nothing is reviewed until you've confirmed.

What Bid Check is — and isn't

  • It is an independent, experienced review of completeness, structure, and market-price reasonableness.
  • It is not professional engineering, architecture, quantity-surveying certification, legal advice, or a guarantee of profit or accuracy. The bid stays yours: you make every final call, and you verify prices with your own suppliers.
  • We review bids from anywhere in the US except where a conflict of interest exists with the reviewer's other professional work; we'll tell you immediately and refund in full if that applies.

Terms (short version, in plain English)

  • Scope: one bid, one written report, one round of follow-up questions by email. Re-reviews after you revise are a new engagement (returning customers: $99).
  • Turnaround: 3 business days from payment and complete documents. Missing documents pause the clock.
  • Confidentiality: your bid and plans are seen only by the reviewer and the AI tooling used to analyze them, and are deleted on request after delivery. They are never shared, sold, or used as content.
  • Refunds: full refund any time before the review starts; if the report doesn't identify at least one finding you consider worth its price, say so within 7 days and we'll refund it.
  • Liability cap: our total liability for anything arising from a review is capped at the fee you paid. You're the contractor of record; bidding decisions and their outcomes are yours.

FAQ

Who's doing the review? A working estimator at a small design-build firm — this is what he does all day, every day. AI tools do the systematic sweeps; experience decides what matters. (More on the About page.)

Why so cheap? Because it's productized: fixed scope, a refined checklist system, and serious tooling. One caught line item usually pays for it several times over.

Can you just do my whole estimate? No — Bid Check reviews your work, it doesn't replace it. That keeps it fast, cheap, and honest.

Is my info safe? Yes — see confidentiality above. We're builders' side, not suppliers' or customers'.
