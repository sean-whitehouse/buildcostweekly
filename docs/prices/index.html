What this means for your bid

Direction: mixed — the two markets disagree this week. Lumber futures (traders' forward view) softened again: down on the week, the month, and about 5.6% below a year ago, with forecasters penciling in roughly flat-to-lower into the fall. But the cash market your yard actually buys from — the Madison's framing composite — is still firm, up 4.4% over the past month. The likely reason: US sawmill capacity is down about 6% year-over-year, so physical supply stays tight even as soft housing demand caps the futures market. Translation: relief may be coming, but it hasn't reached the lumberyard counter yet.

  • Bidding a job that starts within 30 days? Bid off a current written yard quote, not the futures headline. The composite is still creeping up, so today's counter price — not last month's — is what you'll pay.
  • Bidding now for a start 60+ days out? Less reason to over-protect than during a clear run-up: the forward market is pointing sideways-to-down. Still get a written supplier quote with a hold period, but a 2–3% framing contingency is plenty here — you don't need to bid as if prices will keep climbing.
  • Buying for a job already under contract? Nothing says panic-buy — if anything the softer futures argue against front-loading. Buy on your normal schedule.
