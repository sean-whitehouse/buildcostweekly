About BuildCostWeekly

BuildCostWeekly is run by a construction professional who spends his days doing estimating, takeoffs, and project coordination for a small design-build shop — and who got tired of price data written for commodity traders instead of the people actually ordering the lumber.

Every week we read the public price indexes (Madison's/NAHB, futures markets, and others), pull out what actually moved, and translate it into one practical answer: should this change how you bid or buy this week?

How the site is made

The weekly snapshot is compiled with the help of AI tools, working from the public sources linked on every snapshot page, and reviewed by a human before publishing. Guides are written from real-world small-job estimating experience. If we get something wrong, tell us and we'll fix it.

What this site is not

Not financial advice, not a substitute for supplier quotes, and not affiliated with any index provider or lumber dealer. Verify prices with your own suppliers before you commit a number to paper.
